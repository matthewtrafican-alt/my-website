1. Executive Summary

This platform is a career-level workforce development and talent review system designed for HR functions. It connects individual competency development (OJT certification, arc tracking) with organizational talent review (calibration, promotion readiness, standing briefs) through a shared competency ontology. The system is grounded in a behaviorally-anchored competency model, a 36-month career arc framework, and matrix-aware organizational structure. It provides proactive signals — review calendar, leading indicators, ranked priority actions — rather than reactive reporting. All competency content, leveling guidelines, and org structures are configurable for any career field or organization.

2. Problem Statement

Current State — Pain Points

  • Development tracking is role-level, not career-level — proficiency earned in one role is lost at transition
  • Talent reviews are reactive — data assembled on demand, inconsistent quality and preparation burden
  • Matrix org structures create visibility gaps — direct-line and dotted-line leaders hold different views of the same employee
  • Plan vs. actual development tracking does not exist in most HR technology stacks
  • Prescriptive guidance requires manual analysis that rarely happens at the required pace
  • Client perspectives on HRBP effectiveness are collected informally if at all
  • Leading indicators are not surfaced before they become lagging problems

Desired State — Outcomes

  • A single instrument tracking development arc across roles — prior proficiency carries forward with credit
  • Standing briefs always current from live data — talent reviews prepared proactively, not reactively
  • Matrix reporting relationships visible in every view; both lines inform the talent review
  • Plan vs. actual tracked from Day 1 with velocity and variance signals
  • Ranked priority actions surfaced automatically before review gates
  • Client brief available on demand; HR-entered or client-submitted
  • Leading indicators flagged 30/60/90 days before review gates — not after

3. Design Principles

The following principles are non-negotiable and embedded in the architecture. They cannot be overridden by configuration.

#PrincipleImplication
1Individual-driven arcEmployee owns the plan, initiates development, selects career track. Leader enables and certifies — does not own.
2Leader as enabler, not ownerLeader's role: create opportunity, remove blockers, certify milestones, plan transitions.
3Demonstration-based progressionPromotion triggered by demonstrated proficiency against leveling guidelines, not time in role.
4Profile carries across rolesArc is a career instrument. Proficiency transfers with credit at role transitions. History is preserved.
5Matrix org flexibilityTag-based, configurable. No fixed hierarchy. Any employee may carry direct-line and multiple dotted-line relationships.
6Talent pool designation is externalPool membership maintained in a separate system. Not stored in this tool.
7No 360-degree feedbackExplicitly excluded. Expert/Master evidence uses named, gate-based inputs: OJT, leader review, results, client perspective.
8Plain language throughoutNo analytics jargon in user-facing text. Plain business language only.
9Proactive by designSurfaces upcoming reviews and preparation requirements 30/60/90 days before needed.
10Content is replaceableAll competency content, leveling guidelines, and org labels are configurable. HR BP model is a reference implementation.

5. Framework Assumptions (Operating Model)

All parameters are configurable defaults representing the agreed operating model for the HR Business Partner reference implementation.

ParameterDefault ValueNotes
Planning horizon36 monthsAssumed role tenure
Rotation gate18 monthsRotate vs. retain decision point
Foundation target12 monthsInitial entry baseline
Practitioner target24 months from start
Expert / Peak24–36 months
Sustain / Transfer phase36+ monthsKnowledge transfer priority
Lateral hire adjustment6–8 months to FoundationAdjusted from assessed entry proficiency

Four Movement Types (Proficiency Carries with Transfer Credit)

  • Promotion — Vertical. Same domain, higher level. Prior proficiency transfers fully; new tier requirements are net new.
  • Job scope advancement — Horizontal expansion within role. Existing proficiency holds; specific competencies extend.
  • Lateral move — Cross-functional, same level. Partial transfer: domain-agnostic competencies carry fully; functional competencies partially reset.
  • Next target role — Career aspiration that shapes what is prioritized in the current arc.

Two Career Tracks (Individual Selects; Diverge at Expert)

  • Individual Contributor (IC) — Depth, domain authority, technical leadership, industry influence
  • Leadership — Team/function management, organizational scope, people outcomes, enterprise strategy

7. Functional Requirements

7.1 Competency Ontology

Structure and Evidence
FR-01System shall maintain a hierarchical competency model: Domain → Competency → Tier → Required Behaviors → Evidence Tasks
FR-02Each tier shall include: tier label, Required Behaviors (2–4 statements), Impact Criterion, and evidence tasks list
FR-03Each evidence task shall carry: task number, statement, difficulty (1–3), behavior match flag, core/cert/optional designation, context flags (Standard / Deployment / Specialized), proficiency codes for all four career levels
FR-04Proficiency codes — A: Awareness · B: Guided performance · C: Independent · *: Standard required · –: Not applicable
FR-05Each evidence task shall support three-party OJT certification: Training Start, Training Complete, Trainee initials, Trainer initials, Certifier initials. Full certification requires all three initials
FR-06Competency areas shall declare Supporting Competency relationships (dependency links to other nodes)
FR-07All competency content shall be replaceable. HR Business Partner model is a reference implementation only

7.2 Employee Profile

Profile Requirements
FR-09Profile shall include: name, role, function, BU, start date, days in role (calculated), pathway, career track (individual selects), target role, direct-line manager, dotted-line manager(s) with relationship type (business/functional)
FR-10LDP enrollment shall be recorded (program name or null)
FR-11Talent pool designation shall not be stored in this system. A note shall direct users to the external talent review system
FR-12On role transition, profile shall carry forward with prior role history, proficiency credit, and a new arc baseline adjusted for entering proficiency

7.3 Training Architecture Plan

E&T Plan Requirements
FR-13System shall display career development architecture: Build → Growth → Peak → Sustain with organizational impact criteria per phase
FR-14System shall support two training pathways: Initial Entry (12-month Foundation baseline) and Lateral (6–8-month adjusted baseline)
FR-15System shall maintain a Course Objective List (COL) mapping tasks to: Formal Course, Structured OJT, or Self-Study. Gap between COL and task inventory shall be surfaced
FR-16System shall capture Resource Constraints: budget, time, course seat availability, SME access, technology
FR-17System shall display external credential pathway mapping professional certifications to proficiency tiers

7.4 Onboarding & 30/60/90 Framework

Onboarding Requirements
FR-18System shall anchor the development arc to a Day 1 start date per employee — plan baseline for all time-to-productivity calculations
FR-19System shall calculate and display a Time to Productivity timeline showing: plan %, actual %, today marker, milestone markers at Day 30/60/90, 6 months, and 12 months
FR-20System shall display a plan vs. actual variance label: Ahead / On Track / Behind
FR-21System shall provide three checkpoint cards (Day 30, Day 60, Day 90) each containing: theme, "How to Help" support activities, "How to Evaluate" criteria (rubric-referenced), expected %, actual %, and checkpoint rating (Met / Partial / Not Met)
FR-22Checkpoint ratings shall be persisted in browser storage

7.5 Status Reporting

Reporting Requirements
FR-23Lagging: Foundation completion %, avg days to Foundation vs. plan, velocity (tasks/month), 90-day checkpoint pass rate. Leading: velocity trend direction, days since last certification, pending sign-off age, approaching milestone (14-day window). Predictive: projected Foundation completion date at current velocity
FR-24Status report shall support filtering by function and by program/pathway (Initial Entry vs. Lateral)
FR-25Employee status classification: Ahead / On Track / Behind / Stalled (Stalled = no activity in 21+ days)
FR-26System shall display a pending action queue: tasks with Training Complete but absent Certifier initials, with days waiting
FR-27All labels shall use plain business language. No analytics taxonomy in user-facing text

7.6 Talent Review

Talent Review Requirements
FR-28System shall support matrix org — each employee may have one direct-line and one or more dotted-line managers with typed relationships (business / functional)
FR-29Talent review shall be organizable by three axes: org element, direct-line reporting chain, or dotted-line/matrix grouping
FR-30System shall calculate promotion readiness against configured leveling guidelines: Ready / Developing / Not Yet, with specific evidence gaps cited
FR-31System shall detect and flag misalignment when direct-line development assessment and dotted-line/client results assessment diverge materially — surfaced before review meetings
FR-32System shall maintain a review calendar for a 90-day horizon: Immediate (0–30d), Near-Term (30–60d), Planned (60–90d)
FR-33Review types: Direct Leader · Senior Leader · Executive · Client-Requested · Rotation Gate (18-month) · Annual Promotion Gate
FR-34Per upcoming review: title, type, employees on agenda, readiness %, and specific preparation actions
FR-35System shall maintain three standing brief formats always current from live data: Individual brief · Leader brief · Client brief
FR-36Client brief shall support two input modes: HR-entered and Client-submitted. Entry source recorded and displayed
FR-37Talent pool field shall note that pool data is maintained in an external system. No pool data shall be stored in this tool

7.7 Priority Actions (Prescriptive)

Priority Action Requirements
FR-38System shall generate ranked top 3–5 priority actions for: upcoming reviews, at-risk employees, and function-level gaps
FR-39Each action shall include: what to do · who owns it · by when · why it matters · expected impact · underlying assumption
FR-40Actions shall be grouped: Immediate (this week) · Near-Term (this month) · Planned (60–90 days)
FR-41Each action card shall reference the review gate or development event it prepares for
FR-42Priority logic shall weight by: gap severity × competency criticality × employees affected × days until relevant gate

7.8 Data Export & Integration

Integration Requirements
FR-43System shall export all development data as structured JSON with a documented field schema
FR-44System shall export a talent review summary as CSV suitable for BOBJ or similar BI platforms
FR-45JSON schema shall document all fields to support BOBJ universe construction as a supplementary data source alongside HCM master data
FR-46System shall support CSV import of employee roster from HCM systems (SuccessFactors / Workday / SAP HCM)
FR-47Data responsibility separation: Employee master data sourced from HCM. Development data owned by this system
FR-48System shall display JSON schema on demand for integration configuration

8. Non-Functional Requirements

IDRequirement
NFR-01System shall operate entirely client-side with no server dependency
NFR-02All user-generated data shall be persisted in browser localStorage with JSON export/import for portability
NFR-03System shall be deployable as a static site (GitHub Pages, Netlify, or equivalent) with no build process
NFR-04Entire application shall be distributable as a set of HTML files plus a JSON data export
NFR-05No analytics or data science terminology shall be visible to end users
NFR-06All career field-specific content shall be modifiable without changes to core application logic
NFR-07System shall not assume a fixed org hierarchy. Any employee shall support any combination of reporting relationships
NFR-08Talent pool, succession slate, and compensation data shall not be stored or transmitted by this system
NFR-09System shall function in current versions of Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge
