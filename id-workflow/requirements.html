HR Workforce Development & Talent Review Platform

A career-level workforce development and talent review system that connects individual competency development with organizational talent decisions through a shared competency ontology.

Version 1.0 Date June 2026 Status Draft Author Matthew Trafican
1Executive Summary
This platform is a career-level workforce development and talent review system designed for HR functions. It connects individual competency development (OJT certification, arc tracking) with organizational talent review (calibration, promotion readiness, standing briefs) through a shared competency ontology.

The system is grounded in a behaviorally-anchored competency model, a 36-month career arc framework, and matrix-aware organizational structure. It provides proactive signals — review calendar, leading indicators, ranked priority actions — rather than reactive reporting.

The initial deployment covers HR Business Partners as a synthetic reference model. All competency content, leveling guidelines, and org structures are configurable for any career field or organization.
2Problem Statement
Current State — Pain Points
P-01Development tracking is role-level, not career-level — proficiency earned in one role is lost at transition
P-02Talent reviews are reactive — data assembled on demand, inconsistent quality
P-03Matrix org structures create visibility gaps between direct and dotted-line leaders
P-04Plan vs. actual development tracking does not exist in most HR tech stacks
P-05Client perspectives on HRBP effectiveness collected informally if at all
P-06Leading indicators not surfaced until they become lagging problems
Desired State — Outcomes
D-01Development arc tracks across roles; prior proficiency carries forward with credit
D-02Standing briefs always current — talent reviews prepared proactively from live data
D-03Matrix reporting visible in every view; both lines inform the talent review
D-04Plan vs. actual tracked from Day 1 with velocity and variance signals
D-05Client brief available on demand; HR-entered or client-submitted
D-06Leading indicators flagged 30/60/90 days before review gates
3Design Principles
Non-negotiable. These decisions are embedded in the architecture and cannot be overridden by configuration.
Individual-driven arcThe employee owns their plan, initiates development, and selects their career track. The leader enables and certifies — they do not own.
Leader as enabler, not ownerLeader's role: create opportunity, remove blockers, certify milestones, plan transitions. Prescribed actions in the tool reflect this scope.
Demonstration-based progressionPromotion is triggered by demonstrated proficiency against leveling guidelines, not time in role. Time is a floor, not a trigger.
Profile carries across rolesThe arc is a career instrument. Proficiency earned in one role transfers with appropriate credit to the next. History is preserved.
Matrix org flexibilityOrg structure is tag-based and configurable. No fixed hierarchy assumed. Any employee may carry direct-line and multiple dotted-line relationships.
Talent pool designation is externalPool membership and succession slate data carries organizational sensitivity. Maintained in a separate interfacing system. Not stored here.
No 360-degree feedbackExplicitly excluded. Evidence at Expert/Master tier uses named, specific, gate-based inputs: OJT (experience), leader review, results documentation, client perspective.
Plain language throughoutNo analytics jargon in user-facing text. "Where We Are," "Early Signals," "What's at Risk," "Priority Actions" replace analytics taxonomy.
Proactive by designSurfaces upcoming reviews, approaching gates, and preparation requirements 30/60/90 days before needed. Does not wait to be asked.
Content is replaceableAll competency content, leveling guidelines, org labels, and framework parameters are configurable. HR Business Partner model is a reference implementation.

7.1Competency Ontology
Structure and Evidence
FR-01System shall maintain a hierarchical competency model: Domain → Competency → Tier → Required Behaviors → Evidence Tasks
FR-02Each tier shall include: tier label, Required Behaviors (2–4 statements), Impact Criterion, and evidence tasks list
FR-03Each evidence task shall carry: task number, statement, difficulty (1–3), behavior match flag, core/cert/optional designation, context flags (Standard / Deployment / Specialized), and proficiency codes for all four career levels
FR-04Proficiency codes: A = Awareness · B = Guided performance · C = Independent · * = Standard required · – = Not applicable
FR-05Each evidence task shall support three-party OJT certification: Training Start, Training Complete, Trainee initials, Trainer initials, Certifier initials. Full certification requires all three initials present
FR-06Competency areas shall declare Supporting Competency relationships (dependency links to other nodes)
FR-07All competency content shall be replaceable. HR Business Partner model is a reference implementation only
7.2Employee Profile
FR-09Profile shall include: name, role, function, BU, start date, days in role (calculated), pathway, career track (individual selects), target role, direct-line manager, dotted-line manager(s) with relationship type (business/functional)
FR-10LDP enrollment shall be recorded (program name or null)
FR-11Talent pool designation shall not be stored in this system. A note shall direct users to the external talent review system
FR-12On role transition, profile shall carry forward with prior role history, proficiency credit, and a new arc baseline adjusted for entering proficiency
7.3Training Architecture Plan
FR-13System shall display career development architecture: Build → Growth → Peak → Sustain with organizational impact criteria per phase
FR-14System shall support two training pathways: Initial Entry (12-month Foundation baseline) and Lateral (6–8-month adjusted baseline)
FR-15System shall maintain a Course Objective List (COL) mapping tasks to: Formal Course, Structured OJT, or Self-Study. Gap between COL and task inventory shall be surfaced
FR-16System shall capture Resource Constraints: budget, available time, course seat availability, SME access, technology
FR-17System shall display external credential pathway mapping professional certifications (SHRM-CP, SHRM-SCP, ATD CPTD, etc.) to proficiency tiers
7.4Onboarding & 30/60/90 Framework
FR-18System shall anchor the development arc to a Day 1 start date per employee — the plan baseline for all time-to-productivity calculations
FR-19System shall calculate and display a Time to Productivity timeline showing: plan completion %, actual completion %, today marker, milestone markers at Day 30/60/90, 6 months, and 12 months
FR-20System shall display a plan vs. actual variance label: Ahead / On Track / Behind
FR-21System shall provide three checkpoint cards (Day 30, Day 60, Day 90) each containing: theme, "How to Help" support activities, "How to Evaluate" criteria (rubric-referenced), expected %, actual %, and checkpoint rating (Met / Partial / Not Met)
FR-22Checkpoint ratings shall be persisted in browser storage
7.5Status Reporting
FR-23Lagging indicators: Foundation completion %, avg days to Foundation vs. plan, certification velocity (tasks/month), 90-day checkpoint pass rate
Leading indicators: velocity trend direction, days since last certification, pending sign-off age (days), approaching milestone within 14 days
Predictive: projected Foundation completion date at current velocity
FR-24Status report shall support filtering by function and by program/pathway (Initial Entry vs. Lateral)
FR-25System shall classify each employee: Ahead / On Track / Behind / Stalled (Stalled = no certification activity in 21+ days)
FR-26System shall display a pending action queue: tasks where Training Complete is logged but Certifier initials are absent, with days waiting
FR-27All labels shall use plain business language. No analytics taxonomy in user-facing text
7.6Talent Review
FR-28System shall support matrix org — each employee may have one direct-line and one or more dotted-line managers with typed relationships (business / functional)
FR-29Talent review shall be organizable by three axes: org element, direct-line reporting chain, or dotted-line/matrix grouping
FR-30System shall calculate promotion readiness against configured leveling guidelines: Ready / Developing / Not Yet, with specific evidence gaps cited for each determination
FR-31System shall detect and flag misalignment when direct-line development assessment and dotted-line/client results assessment diverge materially — surfaced before reviews, not during them
FR-32System shall maintain a review calendar for a 90-day horizon: Immediate (0–30d), Near-Term (30–60d), Planned (60–90d)
FR-33Review types: Direct Leader · Senior Leader (non-exec) · Executive · Client-Requested · Rotation Gate (18-month) · Annual Promotion Gate
FR-34Per upcoming review: title, type, employees on agenda, readiness %, and specific preparation actions
FR-35System shall maintain three standing brief formats — always current from live data: Individual brief, Leader brief, Client brief
FR-36Client brief shall support two input modes: HR-entered (HR staff enters on behalf of client) and Client-submitted (client accesses directly). Entry source recorded and displayed
FR-37Talent pool field shall display a note directing to the external system. No pool data shall be stored in this tool
7.7Priority Actions (Prescriptive)
FR-38System shall generate ranked top 3–5 priority actions for: upcoming reviews, at-risk employees, and function-level development gaps
FR-39Each action shall include: what to do · who owns it · by when · why it matters · expected impact · underlying assumption
FR-40Actions shall be grouped: Immediate (this week) · Near-Term (this month) · Planned (60–90 days)
FR-41Each action card shall reference the review gate or development event it prepares for
FR-42Priority logic shall weight by: gap severity × competency criticality × employees affected × days until relevant gate
7.8Data Export & Integration
FR-43System shall export all development data as structured JSON with a documented field schema
FR-44System shall export a talent review summary as CSV suitable for BOBJ or similar BI platforms
FR-45JSON schema shall document all fields to support BOBJ universe construction as a supplementary data source alongside HCM master data
FR-46System shall support CSV import of employee roster from HCM systems (SuccessFactors / Workday / SAP HCM)
FR-47Data responsibility separation: Employee master data (name, org, role, start date) is sourced from HCM. Development data (certifications, arc, reviews) is owned by this system
FR-48System shall display JSON schema on demand for integration configuration
8Non-Functional Requirements
NFR-01System shall operate entirely client-side with no server dependency
NFR-02All user-generated data shall be persisted in browser localStorage with JSON export/import for portability
NFR-03System shall be deployable as a static site (GitHub Pages, Netlify, or equivalent) with no build process
NFR-04Entire application shall be distributable as a set of HTML files plus a JSON data export — no installation required
NFR-05No analytics or data science terminology shall be visible to end users
NFR-06All content representing a specific career field shall be modifiable without changes to core application logic
NFR-07System shall not assume a fixed org hierarchy. Any employee shall support any combination of reporting relationships
NFR-08Talent pool, succession slate, and compensation data shall not be stored or transmitted by this system
NFR-09System shall function in current versions of Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge
